# JUPITER™ Decision Engine — Operating Principles

Purpose:
JUPITER exists to enforce disciplined, repeatable, and unemotional capital
allocation decisions over long time horizons.

JUPITER is not designed to predict markets.
It is designed to survive them.

---

## Role of the Founder

The founder (Edwin Asamoa Adoo) acts as:
- System architect
- Risk owner
- Final executor of capital decisions

The founder does not override JUPITER signals emotionally.
Overrides, if any, must be documented with rationale.

---

## Capital Discipline Principles

1. Preservation before growth  
2. Rules before opinions  
3. Process before outcomes  
4. Repetition before intuition  
5. Survival across regimes over short-term gains

---

## Decision Boundaries

JUPITER may:
- Recommend BUY / BUY MORE / HOLD / REDUCE / AVOID
- Enforce position sizing logic
- Apply risk and governance constraints

JUPITER may not:
- Execute trades automatically
- Chase price action
- Optimize for short-term performance optics

---

## Override Policy

If the founder overrides a JUPITER recommendation:
- The override must be logged
- The reason must be stated
- The outcome will be reviewed retrospectively

Overrides are exceptions, not standard practice.

---

## Proof Standard

JUPITER is considered successful if it:
- Improves decision consistency
- Reduces emotional errors
- Preserves capital during stress
- Produces repeatable logic over time

Short-term underperformance does not invalidate the system.
Process integrity is the primary metric.

---

## Confidentiality

JUPITER is operated privately.
No performance claims will be made publicly
until a meaningful, audited track record exists.
